# EXAM_RELEVANCE_GATE.md — Every content decision must help exam performance

Last checked: 2026-05-23

## Primary rule

If this product is positioned as JFT-Basic / SSW preparation, every content decision must be traceable to exam performance or SSW work-life readiness.

Do not create cards just because they are generally useful Japanese.
Do not create cards just because they are easy to translate.
Do not create cards just because they sound practical.

A card must pass the exam-relevance gate.

## Exam-relevance gate

Before a card is accepted, it must answer:

1. Which JFT-Basic section does it support?
   - Script and Vocabulary
   - Conversation and Expression
   - Listening Comprehension
   - Reading Comprehension

2. Which category or skill does it train?
   - word meaning
   - word usage
   - kanji reading
   - kanji meaning and usage
   - grammar in context
   - expression in context
   - conversation comprehension
   - shop/public-place comprehension
   - announcement/instruction comprehension
   - short message/notice reading
   - information search

3. Which real-life or work-life situation does it cover?
   - city office / residence card / documents
   - work schedule / shift
   - health / absence
   - safety / warning / instructions
   - reporting trouble
   - shopping / transport / public place
   - workplace communication
   - housing / dormitory / daily life

4. What mode should train it?
   - recognition: Japanese -> meaning
   - production: support language / situation -> Japanese
   - sign reading: kanji/sign -> reading + meaning
   - listening: Japanese audio -> text + meaning
   - information search: find a needed item in a small notice/table

5. Why would a test-focused learner care?
   - likely to appear as a test skill
   - needed for JFT A1-A2 life-in-Japan tasks
   - supports SSW work/life survival
   - improves confidence in listening/reading question types

If a card cannot answer these, it should be rejected or moved to a later general-Japanese deck.

## Content matrix requirement

Before expanding beyond the current sample, create a content matrix that maps:

- JFT section
- skill category
- life/work domain
- card mode
- target level
- support language
- number of starter cards
- paid-pack potential

This matrix should guide Stage 1B Indonesian + English fallback content.

## Revenue-first reason

Paying users will likely be exam- or qualification-motivated.

Therefore the product must make learners feel:

> This helps me prepare for JFT-Basic / SSW and also helps me survive real Japanese situations in Japan.

A generic practical Japanese card is not enough.
A test-relevant practical card is the target.

## Recommended Stage 1B allocation

For an initial 60-card Indonesian validation sample, use a balanced exam-facing allocation:

- Script and Vocabulary: 14 cards
  - word meaning
  - word usage
  - kanji reading
  - kanji meaning and usage

- Conversation and Expression: 14 cards
  - grammar in context
  - expression in context
  - common requests, confirmations, apologies, reports

- Listening-ready cards: 14 cards
  - short instructions
  - announcements
  - workplace/public-place exchanges
  - no audio required at first, but fields should be audio-ready

- Reading / Information Search: 12 cards
  - notices
  - messages
  - schedules
  - signs
  - simple information tables

- SSW workplace survival overlay: 6 cards
  - safety
  - reporting a problem
  - health/absence
  - shift/schedule
  - supervisor communication

This is a hypothesis, not a final curriculum. Adjust after the exam blueprint and validation feedback.

## Source discipline

Use public/official sources to infer structure and skill domains:

- JFT-Basic official test structure
- JFT-Basic sample questions and test description
- Irodori / Japanese for Life in Japan scope and lesson/category structure
- JF Can-do descriptions where useful
- SSW public work/life context

Do not copy:

- official question text
- official choices
- audio scripts
- textbook dialogues
- recalled or leaked exam questions

## Operational instruction for ChatGPT and Codex

Do not wait for the owner to point out exam relevance.

Before proposing new content, ChatGPT/Codex should proactively state:

- which exam section it supports
- why the learner would pay for it
- whether it belongs in free sample or paid pack
- what evidence is needed next

## Success condition

A learner or institution should be able to look at the free sample and think:

> This is not just a Japanese app. It is organized around the skills I need for JFT-Basic / SSW.

That perception is required for monetization.
