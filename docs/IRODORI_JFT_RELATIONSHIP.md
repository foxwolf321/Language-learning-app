# IRODORI_JFT_RELATIONSHIP.md — How Irodori relates to JFT-Basic content

Last checked: 2026-05-23

## Bottom line

Irodori should be treated as a strong indicator of the **skill domains, level, and real-life situations** that matter for JFT-Basic / SSW preparation.

It should **not** be treated as a source of exact test questions.

## What is likely

Because both JFT-Basic and Irodori are connected to the Japan Foundation's A1-A2 / Can-do / life-in-Japan framework, there is likely substantial overlap in:

- level range
- communicative skills
- daily-life domains
- work/life situations
- vocabulary and grammar bands
- listening/reading task types
- practical scenario categories

This means Irodori is useful for deciding what kinds of original cards to create.

## What is not safe to assume

Do not assume:

- JFT-Basic questions are directly taken from Irodori.
- Irodori sentences will appear as exam questions.
- Irodori dialogues, audio scripts, or exercises can be copied into the app.
- Memorizing Irodori text equals exam prediction.

## Product use

Use Irodori to infer categories such as:

- greetings and self-introduction
- daily routines
- shopping and public places
- transport and locations
- health and absence
- city office and procedures
- workplace communication
- requests, confirmation, reporting
- instructions and announcements
- notices and information search

Then create original JFT/SSW-aligned cards.

## Marketing implication

The app should not say:

- "Questions come from Irodori."
- "Memorize these and the same questions will appear."

It can say, if appropriate:

- "Aligned with JFT-Basic / SSW practical communication needs."
- "Covers daily-life and work-in-Japan situations at A1-A2 level."
- "Original cards for exam-oriented practice and real-life preparation."

## Content rule

Irodori is a map, not a mine.

Use it to understand the territory.
Do not extract its text as app content.

## Revenue relevance

For monetization, the product must feel useful for exam-oriented learners. Irodori helps us identify high-value domains, but the paid app should stand on original, legally safe, mobile-first card content.
