# CARD_COUNT_STRATEGY.md — How many cards are enough?

Last checked: 2026-05-23

## Primary answer

For a monetizable JFT-Basic / SSW product, the target should not be "a few dozen cards".

Recommended scale:

| Product stage | Approx. cards | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Stage 1A technical sample | 50 | Verify PWA and card flow. |
| Stage 1B Indonesian validation sample | 60-80 | Show exam-aligned value and validate Indonesian + English fallback. |
| First paid starter product | 300-500 | Enough to feel like a serious JFT/SSW prep product. |
| Full JFT/SSW core product | 800-1,200 card exposures | Broad enough for exam preparation across the four JFT sections and work-life readiness. |
| Field-specific paid pack | +150-300 per field | Nursing care, manufacturing/safety, food service, cleaning, etc. |

## Important distinction: items vs cards

Do not blindly make four cards from every Japanese sentence.

Use two concepts:

1. **Core learning item**
   - one Japanese phrase, word, sign, notice, audio script, or scenario.

2. **Card exposure**
   - one practice direction/mode generated from that item.

Example core item:

- 機械が止まりました。

Possible card exposures:

- Recognition: Japanese -> Indonesian/English meaning
- Production: Indonesian/English situation -> Japanese
- Listening: Japanese audio -> meaning/text

But not every item needs every mode.

## Four-mode strategy

The product has four key modes:

1. Recognition / Reading
   - Japanese -> meaning
   - default for many starter cards

2. Production
   - support-language situation -> Japanese
   - useful for speaking/workplace readiness

3. Kanji / Sign Reading
   - kanji/sign/notice -> reading + meaning
   - useful for reading section and real life

4. Listening
   - Japanese audio -> text/meaning
   - high value, likely paid differentiator

## Recommended full core size

### Core learning items

A strong full core should likely contain about:

- 450-650 unique learning items

These should include:

- words
- phrases
- short sentences
- signs/notices
- mini-scenarios
- listening-ready scripts
- simple information-search tasks

### Card exposures

After mode expansion, this becomes roughly:

- 800-1,200 card exposures

This feels large enough to be a real product but not so large that production quality collapses.

## Suggested full core allocation

For an 900-card-exposure full core:

| Area | Approx. card exposures | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Script and Vocabulary | 180-220 | Word meaning, word usage, kanji reading, kanji meaning/usage. |
| Conversation and Expression | 180-220 | Grammar/expression in context; requests, confirmation, apology, reporting. |
| Listening-ready / audio cards | 180-220 | Conversations, instructions, announcements; audio can be added later. |
| Reading / Information Search | 150-200 | Notices, messages, signs, schedules, forms, tables. |
| SSW workplace survival overlay | 120-180 | Safety, reporting, shift, health, supervisor communication; overlaps with the above but should be tracked. |
| Review / mixed exam-style cards | 50-100 | Integrative cards and weak-point practice. |

## Why this scale makes sense

JFT-Basic has around 50 questions in 60 minutes and is organized into four sections: Script and Vocabulary, Conversation and Expression, Listening Comprehension, and Reading Comprehension, each with around 12 questions. A 60-card sample can show value, but it cannot provide enough repetition and variation for serious preparation.

A paid product should give multiple practice items for each section/category, otherwise it will feel like a demo rather than preparation.

## Stage 1B Indonesian validation size

60-80 cards is enough for validation if the cards are carefully chosen.

Recommended 60-card allocation:

- Script and Vocabulary: 14
- Conversation and Expression: 14
- Listening-ready: 14
- Reading / Information Search: 12
- SSW workplace survival overlay: 6

This is not enough as a final paid product, but it is enough to test:

- whether Indonesian support matters
- whether English fallback is useful
- whether the content feels exam-relevant
- whether users want a full paid pack

## First paid product size

The first paid product should probably be:

- 300-500 cards

Recommended paid starter allocation:

- Script and Vocabulary: 70-100
- Conversation and Expression: 70-100
- Listening-ready: 70-100
- Reading / Information Search: 60-90
- SSW workplace survival: 50-80

This gives enough density to sell as a starter pack without requiring a full year of production.

## Full paid product size

A full JFT/SSW core product should likely be:

- 800-1,200 card exposures
- about 450-650 unique learning items

This can be positioned as:

- complete starter-to-JFT core
- JFT/SSW practical Japanese core
- Work-in-Japan Japanese core deck

## Field-specific expansion

After the core product, do not simply add more generic Japanese.

Add paid vertical packs:

| Pack | Approx. additional cards |
|---|---:|
| Nursing care Japanese | 200-300 |
| Manufacturing / safety Japanese | 200-300 |
| Food service / customer Japanese | 150-250 |
| Building cleaning Japanese | 150-250 |
| Agriculture Japanese | 150-250 |
| Interview / onboarding Japanese | 150-250 |

These packs may monetize better than a huge generic deck because the buyer can see a direct job-related benefit.

## What not to do

Do not build:

- 2,000+ cards before validation
- four modes for every item automatically
- all countries/languages at once
- audio for every card before paid-demand evidence
- low-quality filler cards just to increase count

A smaller high-relevance paid product is better than a huge vague deck.

## Revenue-first recommendation

1. Create 60-80 Stage 1B Indonesian validation cards.
2. If response is positive, expand to a 300-500 card paid starter.
3. If paid starter works, build the 800-1,200 card full core.
4. Add field-specific packs only after seeing which worker segments respond.

## Quality threshold

Every card must pass the exam relevance gate:

- Which JFT section?
- Which skill category?
- Which life/work situation?
- Which card mode?
- Why would a test-focused learner care?

If a card cannot answer these, it should not be included just to raise the count.
