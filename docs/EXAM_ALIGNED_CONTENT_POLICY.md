# EXAM_ALIGNED_CONTENT_POLICY.md — Exam-aligned, not copied

Last checked: 2026-05-23

## Core principle

The likely paying user is exam- or work-qualification motivated.

Therefore the product should not be marketed as a vague practical Japanese app. It should be positioned as:

> JFT-Basic / SSW practical Japanese preparation that is aligned with exam goals and real work-life needs, while using original card content rather than copied official questions or textbook sentences.

## Important distinction

Do not say:

- "This is not exam preparation."
- "This is only practical Japanese."
- "We do not care about JFT-Basic or SSW tests."

Instead say prominently:

- "Built for JFT-Basic / SSW preparation."
- "Exam-aligned practical Japanese."
- "Original cards based on the skills and situations needed for the exam and for work/life in Japan."

Do **not** overemphasize legal disclaimers in marketing copy. A normal study aid or reference book does not put disclaimers at the center of the sales message. Keep necessary cautionary language in small footer text, FAQ, terms, or internal policy.

Recommended treatment:

- Main copy: focus on exam preparation, practical work/life Japanese, and learner benefit.
- Footer/FAQ/terms: short note if needed that the product is an independent learning aid.
- Internal policy: preserve the rule that content is original and not copied from official questions or textbooks.

Avoid prominent negative phrases such as:

- "No pass guarantee" as a main CTA-area message.
- "Not affiliated with official exam bodies" as a headline-level message.
- "Not copied from official questions" as a selling point.

These ideas are operational guardrails, not the value proposition.

## Why original content is still necessary

The product can be exam-focused without copying official or copyrighted material.

What we can use:

- official exam descriptions
- test sections and skill areas
- level descriptions
- public sample question style analysis
- official can-do style descriptions
- public implementation reports
- field/work-life categories

What we should not use:

- copied official questions
- copied answer choices
- copied audio scripts
- copied textbook sentences/dialogues
- recalled or leaked test questions
- paid textbook examples copied into cards

## Buyer psychology

A learner may pay because they want:

- to pass JFT-Basic
- to satisfy SSW-related Japanese requirements
- to feel safer before coming to Japan
- to understand workplace instructions
- to handle city office / residence / schedule / health situations
- to practice daily on a phone
- to get native-language explanations and Japanese audio

The landing page should speak directly to this motivation.

## Product positioning

Recommended product phrase:

> Work-in-Japan Japanese cards for JFT-Basic / SSW preparation.

Alternative phrases:

- JFT-Basic practical Japanese starter
- SSW workplace Japanese starter
- Japanese for working in Japan
- JFT/SSW Japanese cards with Indonesian support and English fallback

Avoid overly generic phrases:

- Japanese flashcards
- practical Japanese app
- language learning app

These are too broad and weak for conversion.

## Content design rule

Every card should map to at least one exam/work-life purpose:

- JFT-Basic daily-life communication
- SSW workplace communication
- recognition of common Japanese phrases
- production of common Japanese responses
- sign/kanji reading
- listening comprehension
- workplace safety
- city office / residence procedures
- schedule and shift communication
- health and absence
- reporting a problem
- polite requests

## Card metadata recommendation

Future card data should include fields such as:

```json
{
  "exam_alignment": ["jft_basic", "ssw_work_life"],
  "skill_area": ["reading", "listening", "conversation_expression"],
  "scenario": "city_office_residence_card",
  "mode": "recognition",
  "source_type": "original_exam_aligned",
  "source_note": "Original card based on public exam skill descriptions and common work/life situation. Not copied from official questions or textbooks."
}
```

## Stage 1B implication

For Indonesian Stage 1B, the goal should be:

- not merely Indonesian translation
- not merely practical Japanese
- but exam-aligned JFT/SSW starter cards

A good free sample should make the learner think:

> This helps me prepare for JFT-Basic/SSW and also helps me understand real Japanese I may use in Japan.

## Monetization relevance

If the app does not connect to the learner's exam/qualification goal, willingness to pay may be weak.

If the app copies official/test material, future monetization is risky.

The correct path is:

> exam-aligned promise + original content + practical work-life usefulness.

Legal/cautionary language should protect the product quietly, not weaken the sales message.
