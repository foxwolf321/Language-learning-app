# EXAM_CONTENT_BLUEPRINT.md

Last checked: 2026-05-24

## 1. Executive summary

This blueprint exists for a revenue-first reason: the product must be perceived as a focused preparation tool for a painful, paid problem, not as a vague Japanese flashcard app. The positioning should be:

> "JFT-Basic / SSW practical Japanese preparation for working and living in Japan"

The current product thesis is JFT-Basic / SSW / Work-in-Japan practical Japanese cards for Indonesian learners, with Indonesian support and English fallback. This document is the planning step before writing Stage 1B cards. It should guide Issue #14, not create card content in Issue #13.

Evidence-backed claims:

- JFT-Basic is organized around four public sections: Script and Vocabulary, Conversation and Expression, Listening Comprehension, and Reading Comprehension.[^jft-about]
- The public structure says each section has around 12 questions, with about 50 questions total and 60 minutes overall.[^jft-about]
- JFT-Basic targets everyday-life Japanese needed by non-native speakers, especially people coming to Japan mainly for work, and is used for Specified Skilled Worker (i) Japanese-language qualification purposes.[^jft-about][^ssw-steps]
- Irodori and JF Can-do for Life in Japan are useful public maps for A1-A2 life/work communication domains, but not card text sources.[^irodori-about][^jf-cando-life]

Product assumptions:

- Indonesian should be the first Stage 1B validation lane because Indonesia has the strongest JFT-Basic examinee signal in the repo's existing research, and Indonesian support is lower-cost than several non-Latin-script options.
- A 60-card validation sample is large enough to test positioning and small enough to review.
- Listening-ready and information-search cards should be included before audio, because they make the free sample feel exam-relevant without adding production cost.

Uncertainties:

- Public sources do not provide a full past-question frequency database, so this blueprint does not claim trend certainty.
- A blueprint does not guarantee exam success or revenue.
- Paid demand still needs validation with learners, schools, sending organizations, registered support organizations, or employers.

## 2. Source discipline

The public sources were used only to infer test structure, skill areas, domain categories, task types, level expectations, and high-value life/work situations. They must not be used as a source of copied learner-facing text.

| Source type | Use allowed | Use forbidden | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| JFT-Basic official About / test structure pages | Test purpose, target learners, CBT method, sections, categories, approximate counts, time limit, level framing. | Copying page wording into cards or implying official endorsement. | Strongest source for initial JFT section coverage. |
| JFT-Basic official sample questions | Infer question format, task shape, section/category coverage, and level feel. | Copying question text, choices, images, audio scripts, scenarios, or making near-clones. | Use as format signal only. Do not mine as content. |
| JFT-Basic Document Describing the Test | Confirm test structure, CBT behavior, local-language prompt support, section categories, and sample-format range. | Copying sample content, choices, prompts, or scripts. | Useful for app design and metadata, not card writing. |
| JFT-Basic implementation reports | Country prioritization and validation context. | Inventing market revenue statistics from examinee counts. | Examinee volume is a targeting signal, not proof of willingness to pay. |
| Irodori / Japanese for Life in Japan | A1-A2 life/work topic map, lesson/category structure, Can-do-oriented scope. | Copying dialogues, exercise sentences, lesson text, word lists, audio scripts, or examples. | Treat as a map, not a mine. |
| JF Can-do / Life in Japan | Communicative task domains and A1-A2 life-in-Japan descriptors. | Copying descriptors as card prompts or treating every descriptor as a final card. | Use to group situations and modes. |
| SSW official information | Work-in-Japan context, qualification path, field awareness, documents/procedures, support context. | Copying official forms, proprietary manuals, or field test questions into cards. | Use field context to plan paid packs later. |
| Public preparation articles/videos | Weak secondary hints about learner anxiety or perceived difficulty. | Using them as source text, past-question evidence, or authority over official sources. | Not relied on for card content in this issue. |

Original card writing should use only structure/domain insight. For example, if a public source shows that information search from notices is a JFT task type, the future card writer should create a new original notice/table and a new learner task. The source should never be paraphrased into the app as a disguised copy.

## 3. JFT-Basic test structure

JFT-Basic is a computer-based test. The official About page states that questions are answered on computers, with audio played through headphones for listening tasks.[^jft-about] The test structure is public and high-level enough to guide card metadata without copying questions.

| Section | Approx. question count | Skill areas | What the section appears to test | Card design implication |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Script and Vocabulary | Around 12 | Word meaning, word usage, kanji reading, kanji meaning and usage. | Whether the learner can read everyday Japanese text, understand basic vocabulary, and use words in context. | Recognition cards, short usage cards, kanji/sign reading cards. |
| Conversation and Expression | Around 12 | Grammar in context, expression in context. | Whether the learner can choose grammar and expressions that match everyday conversation situations. | Production cards from Indonesian/English situations, plus context recognition. |
| Listening Comprehension | Around 12 | Short conversations, shop/public-place exchanges, announcements and instructions. | Whether the learner can understand spoken everyday Japanese and identify key content, action, place, time, or intent. | Listening-ready text first; audio later after cost gate. |
| Reading Comprehension | Around 12 | Short messages/notices, reading content, information search. | Whether the learner can understand simple written information and find needed details in notices/materials. | Reading mini cards, sign reading, information-search mini cards. |

Other public structure notes:

- The official page states about 50 questions total and 60 minutes overall.[^jft-about]
- The current level framing is A2, with A1-A2 Can-do-based question design.[^jft-about]
- Official notice says from August 2026 JFT-Basic will also report A1 and A2.1 assessment levels, but says the testing method, structure, and question level do not change.[^jft-notice]
- JFT-Basic does not need speaking or writing cards for direct test-format replication, but production cards remain commercially useful because SSW/work-life learners need to say things at work.

No official sample question text, choices, or audio scripts are reproduced here.

## 6. Card-type mapping

Use these card modes in future content work:

1. Recognition: Japanese -> Indonesian/English meaning.
2. Production: Indonesian/English situation prompt -> Japanese.
3. Kanji / Sign Reading: kanji/sign/notice -> reading + meaning.
4. Listening-ready: Japanese audio later -> text + meaning. For now, write audio-ready text only; do not add audio files.
5. Information-search mini card: learner finds a needed item from a short notice/table/message.

The examples below are content-type ideas only, not production card drafts.

| JFT section / skill | App card mode | Example original content type | Why learner would care |
|---|---|---|---|
| Script and Vocabulary / word meaning | Recognition | A workplace or city-office word shown in Japanese, learner checks Indonesian meaning and English fallback. | Builds fast recognition for exam vocabulary and real notices. |
| Script and Vocabulary / word usage | Recognition | A short original sentence with a missing or highlighted practical word, no copied source phrasing. | Trains usage, not just dictionary meaning. |
| Script and Vocabulary / kanji reading | Kanji / Sign Reading | A common life/work kanji word or sign label with kana and meaning on the back. | Helps with public signs, forms, and JFT script tasks. |
| Script and Vocabulary / kanji meaning and usage | Kanji / Sign Reading | A short original sign/notice fragment where the learner identifies meaning and use. | Connects kanji to practical reading. |
| Conversation and Expression / grammar in context | Production | Indonesian situation asks learner to produce a short Japanese request, confirmation, or reason. | Helps both exam context-choice and work-life speaking confidence. |
| Conversation and Expression / expression in context | Production | Learner chooses or recalls a polite expression for asking, apologizing, reporting, or confirming. | High emotional value for workers afraid of saying the wrong thing. |
| Listening / short conversation | Listening-ready | Original two-turn exchange seed about time, place, next action, or workplace task. | Prepares for one-pass comprehension before audio exists. |
| Listening / shop or public place | Listening-ready | Original staff/customer or counter exchange seed. | Public-place listening is a visible JFT skill and daily survival need. |
| Listening / announcements and instructions | Listening-ready | Original short announcement/instruction seed about schedule, safety, or location. | Directly supports listening to instructions and workplace announcements. |
| Reading / short messages or notices | Information-search mini card | Original short message where learner finds one needed date/time/place/action. | Mirrors the skill of finding useful information without copying official materials. |
| Reading / signs | Kanji / Sign Reading | Common public/workplace sign category with reading and meaning. | Useful for safety, public places, and fast visual recognition. |
| SSW work-life overlay | Production; Listening-ready; Kanji / Sign Reading | Original field-neutral problem report, safety warning, absence, or shift scenario. | Makes the product feel useful for working in Japan, not just passing a test. |

## 8. Indonesian + English fallback implications

Stage 1B should guide Indonesian-first validation without building full multilingual support.

- Indonesian should be the main support language for the validation sample.
- English should remain as fallback/reference for QA, teachers, mixed-language learners, and uncertain translations.
- Indonesian translations must be marked as draft / needs human review until checked by a fluent reviewer.
- Japanese target text should be short, natural, and A1-A2/JFT-ish.
- Romaji can exist for early learners but should be optional/fadeable.
- Kana reading should remain available for kanji and signs.
- Do not build full multilingual support yet.
- Do not add support-language audio yet.

Product implication: the next content files in Issue #14 should be designed so that Indonesian appears as the learner-facing support language, English remains a stable reference, and translation review status is explicit.

- Irodori/JF Can-do domain mapping
- SSW work-life relevance
- real user performance data

Starting implication: because public structure is roughly balanced across four sections, the first scheduler can begin with broad section balance. Listening-ready, reading/information-search, and workplace safety/reporting may receive modest product-assumption boosts, but those boosts must be revised after real learner data.

## 10. Content acceptance gate for future cards

Every Stage 1B card must pass this checklist before it is accepted into Issue #14 outputs:

- Which JFT section does it support?
- Which skill category does it train?
- Which life/work situation does it cover?
- Which mode trains it?
- Why would a test-focused learner care?
- Is the Japanese original and natural?
- Is it short enough for A1-A2/JFT-Basic-ish learners?
- Is Indonesian marked as draft unless reviewed?
- Is it free-sample-worthy or should it be paid-pack later?
- Is there any risk of copying official/textbook material?

Reject or postpone any card that cannot answer these clearly.
