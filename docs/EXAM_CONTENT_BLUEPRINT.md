# EXAM_CONTENT_BLUEPRINT.md

Last checked: 2026-05-24

## Purpose

This blueprint turns public JFT-Basic / SSW / Irodori / JF Can-do source analysis into a safe content plan for Indonesian Stage 1B card writing.

The product should feel clearly useful for JFT-Basic / SSW preparation, but all app cards must be original. This document is a planning artifact only: do not add cards, audio, or PWA changes as part of this task.

## Source Discipline

Use public sources to infer structure, skills, levels, and situation categories. Do not use source text as card text.

Allowed source use:

- Official JFT-Basic pages: test purpose, target users, CBT structure, sections, categories, time, score context.
- Official JFT-Basic sample questions and document describing the test: question formats, category coverage, situation types, and level signal only.
- JFT-Basic implementation reports: country/language prioritization and validation context.
- Irodori overview and table-of-contents/category structure: practical A1-A2 life/work topic map only.
- JF Can-do for Life in Japan: communicative-task categories and A1-A2 life/work scope.
- SSW official pages: work-in-Japan context, Japanese-language test requirement, field-adjacent communication needs.

Blocked source use:

- Do not copy official sample question wording, answer choices, images, or audio scripts.
- Do not copy Irodori dialogues, lesson sentences, exercises, word lists, audio scripts, or textbook examples.
- Do not use recalled questions, leaked questions, unofficial past-question collections, or paid textbook examples.
- Do not claim access to unpublished past exams or real test trends beyond public sources.

Important public-source caveat: JFT-Basic FAQ says past questions are not currently planned for publication; sample questions are provided instead.[^jft-faq] Therefore this blueprint is based on public structure and sample-format analysis, not real past-exam mining.

## 1. Test Structure

JFT-Basic is a CBT test for non-native Japanese speakers, especially people coming to Japan mainly for work. The official purpose is to measure Japanese communication needed in daily-life situations in Japan and to judge whether the learner can handle everyday conversation/life at a certain level.[^jft-about]

Officially relevant structure:

| Section | Approx. count | What it tests | Public category signal | App implication |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Script and Vocabulary | Around 12 | Reading everyday Japanese text, basic vocabulary knowledge, and usage. | Word meaning; word usage; kanji reading; kanji meaning and usage.[^jft-about] | Recognition cards, kanji/sign-reading cards, short usage-in-context cards. |
| Conversation and Expression | Around 12 | Grammar and expressions needed for everyday conversation. | Grammar matching context; expression matching context.[^jft-about] | Production cards, context-completion cards, polite request/reporting cards. |
| Listening Comprehension | Around 12 | Understanding conversations, exchanges, announcements, and instructions in everyday situations. | Conversation; shops/public places; announcements/instructions.[^jft-about] | Listening-ready cards, audio-later cards, instruction/announcement comprehension cards. |
| Reading Comprehension | Around 12 | Reading letters, notices, explanations, and information materials in everyday situations. | Comprehending content; information search.[^jft-about] | Notice/message cards, sign-reading cards, information-search mini cards. |

Additional constraints:

- Total: approximately 50 questions, 60 minutes.[^jft-about]
- Current official test level target: A2, with A1-A2 Can-do basis in the question design.[^jft-faq]
- No oral test or essay/writing test currently.[^jft-faq]
- Listening review behavior differs from other sections; learners need one-pass listening confidence.[^jft-about]

Product interpretation: the app should not become a speaking-output-only phrasebook or a generic vocabulary deck. It should cover all four JFT sections, while still preserving SSW work/life usefulness.

## 2. Likely High-Value Content Domains

These are category/domain inferences, not copied content. They come from official section purposes, the public test-description PDF, JF Can-do categories, Irodori scope, and SSW work context.

### Script / Vocabulary

High-value domains:

- kana stability: hiragana, katakana, long vowels, small tsu, loanword recognition.
- daily-life vocabulary: residence, address, city office, health, transport, shopping, time, numbers, dates.
- workplace vocabulary: work, shift, break, supervisor, report, tool, machine, material, safety, confirmation.
- kanji reading: common life/work words that appear in notices, forms, schedules, safety signs, and public information.
- kanji meaning and usage: choosing the right kanji word in a short practical sentence.

Card direction:

- Keep Japanese targets short and A1-A2 practical.
- Use kanji only when the learner needs to recognize the word in real life; provide kana reading on the back or as hidden help.
- Avoid filler vocabulary with weak life/work relevance.

### Conversation / Expression

High-value domains:

- greetings, self-introduction, workplace first-day phrases.
- asking for repetition, clarification, permission, help, and slower speech.
- reporting problems: machine stopped, material shortage, injury, illness, being late.
- polite requests and responses: asking for a day off, confirming instructions, checking schedule.
- context grammar: time, reason, sequence, condition, prohibition, obligation, and simple cause/result.

Card direction:

- Production mode should be prominent for this section: Indonesian situation -> Japanese.
- Recognition mode still matters, because learners must choose context-appropriate grammar/expression in the exam.
- Write situations generically; do not imitate official dialogue setups.

### Listening

High-value domains:

- short work or daily-life conversations where the learner must identify main action, place, time, speaker intent, or next step.
- shop/public-place exchanges: asking staff, choosing an item, confirming availability, understanding restrictions.
- announcements and instructions: workplace announcements, emergency drills, schedule changes, safety directions.
- one-pass comprehension: numbers, times, locations, sequence words, negative/prohibition words, and urgent action verbs.

Card direction:

- Stage 1B should be listening-ready but text-first: no full audio production yet.
- Each listening-ready card should include an original Japanese script seed, but actual audio can remain null until the audio cost gate is passed.
- Do not copy public sample audio scripts or Irodori listening scripts.

### Reading / Information Search

High-value domains:

- short messages, notices, public information, city-office reminders, simple workplace notes.
- signs and labels: emergency exit, danger, no entry, reception, break room, office, schedule board.
- tables and structured text: shift schedule, opening hours, bus/train times, trash collection days, medicine instructions.
- information search: find needed date/time/place/action from a small, original notice or mini table.

Card direction:

- Use original mini-notices and tables, not copied source layouts.
- Keep one task per card: find time, find place, find required item, or identify prohibited action.
- These cards are especially useful for perceived exam relevance because they map directly to Reading Comprehension.

### SSW Work/Life Overlay

SSW official pages frame SSW(i) as requiring a Japanese-language test and a skills proficiency test unless exempted by qualifying training history.[^ssw-tests] The general Japanese product should stay field-neutral at Stage 1B, but it can include common work/life situations that apply across fields.

High-value domains:

- city office / residence / documents.
- workplace schedule / shift / break.
- health / absence / reporting illness.
- safety / warning / emergency / instructions.
- problem reporting and supervisor communication.
- shopping / transport / public places.

Field-specific paid packs can come later; Stage 1B should not overfit nursing care, manufacturing, food service, or cleaning yet.

## 4. Stage 1B Indonesian Starter Blueprint

Recommended Stage 1B size: 60 cards.

Why 60:

- Large enough to show that the product is exam-aligned, not only a phrase demo.
- Small enough to translate, review, and rewrite after validation.
- Consistent with the current card-count strategy for Indonesian + English fallback.
- Does not pretend to be a full paid prep product.

Support language rule:

- Primary support: Indonesian draft text.
- Fallback: English reference text.
- Translation status: Indonesian must be marked machine_draft or needs_human_review until checked by a fluent reviewer.
- UI implication later: Indonesian first, English fallback optional/collapsible.

### 60-card Composition

| Area | Count | Modes | Source-aligned skill coverage | Domain targets |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Script and Vocabulary | 14 | Recognition; kanji/sign-reading | Word meaning, word usage, kanji reading, kanji meaning/use. | documents, city office, time/date, health, transport, safety, workplace objects. |
| Conversation and Expression | 14 | Production; recognition | Grammar in context, expression in context. | clarification, permission, polite request, apology, report, confirmation, schedule. |
| Listening-ready phrases | 14 | Listening-ready; recognition | Conversation, shops/public places, announcements/instructions. | staff exchange, workplace announcement, emergency/safety instruction, schedule change. |
| Reading / Information Search | 12 | Reading mini-card; sign-reading; information-search | Short messages/notices, everyday information search. | shift board, trash day, medicine note, public notice, city-office reminder, sign/label. |
| SSW workplace survival overlay | 6 | Mixed: production, listening-ready, sign-reading | Common field-neutral work/life readiness. | safety, problem reporting, health/absence, supervisor communication, break/shift. |

### Detailed Stage 1B Allocation

Script and Vocabulary, 14 cards:

- 3 kana/katakana cards for work/life loanwords and public-place words.
- 4 core daily-life words: residence/document/address/health/transport cluster.
- 3 workplace words: work/task/tool/material/supervisor cluster.
- 2 kanji-reading cards for public-life words.
- 2 kanji/sign meaning cards for safety or facility words.

Conversation and Expression, 14 cards:

- 3 clarification/repetition cards.
- 3 polite request cards.
- 2 permission cards.
- 3 reporting/problem cards.
- 2 schedule/time confirmation cards.
- 1 apology/thanks/context repair card.

Listening-ready, 14 cards:

- 4 short workplace instruction seeds.
- 3 public-place/staff exchange seeds.
- 3 announcement/instruction seeds.
- 2 emergency/safety instruction seeds.
- 2 schedule/time/number listening seeds.

Reading / Information Search, 12 cards:

- 3 short message cards.
- 3 sign/label cards.
- 3 schedule/table cards.
- 2 city-office/public notice cards.
- 1 medicine/health instruction card.

SSW workplace survival overlay, 6 cards:

- 2 safety/prohibition cards.
- 2 problem-reporting cards.
- 1 health/absence card.
- 1 supervisor/shift communication card.

Optional stretch to 80 cards after feedback:

- Add 5 more information-search mini cards.
- Add 5 more listening-ready announcement/instruction cards.
- Add 5 production cards for workplace reporting and polite requests.
- Add 5 kanji/sign-reading cards for city-office, safety, and schedule words.

## 5. Content Creation Rules

### Originality Rules

Every Stage 1B card must be original.

A card may be inspired by a public category such as `information search` or `listening to announcements and instructions`, but the actual Japanese sentence, Indonesian translation, English fallback, example, and audio script seed must be newly written.

Do not create near-clones of sample questions by swapping names, places, times, products, or answer choices. If the source scenario feels too close, change the task type and domain before writing.

### Metadata Rules

Future Stage 1B cards should include or be ready for:

```json
{
  "exam_alignment": ["jft_basic", "ssw_work_life"],
  "exam_section": "script_vocabulary",
  "skill_category": "kanji_reading",
  "life_work_domain": "city_office_documents",
  "mode": "kanji_sign_reading",
  "support_language": "id",
  "fallback_language": "en",
  "translation_status": {
    "id": "machine_draft",
    "en": "source_or_reviewed"
  },
  "audio": {
    "ja_target": null,
    "ja_example": null,
    "ja_listening": null,
    "support_language_optional": null
  },
  "source_note": "Original card based on public exam section/category and life/work domain analysis. Not copied from official questions, Irodori, textbooks, recalled questions, or audio scripts."
}
```

### Language Rules

- Indonesian support should be plain, practical, and learner-friendly.
- English fallback should remain simple and literal enough for QA.
- Japanese should be natural but controlled for A1-A2 / JFT-Basic-ish learners.
- Romaji should be optional/fadeable, not the core learning surface.
- Kana reading should be available for kanji-heavy or sign-reading cards.

### Audio Rules

- Stage 1B text cards should be audio-ready but not audio-dependent.
- Do not add audio files yet.
- Do not write support-language audio scripts yet.
- If later testing adds 3-5 audio samples, use newly written Japanese target phrases only.

### Quality Gate

Before a Stage 1B card is accepted, it must answer:

1. Which JFT section does it support?
2. Which category does it train?
3. Which life/work domain does it cover?
4. Which card mode does it use?
5. Why would a JFT/SSW-focused learner care?
6. Is every learner-facing sentence original?
7. Is Indonesian marked as draft until human review?
8. Could this be sold later without copyright or official-affiliation risk?

## 6. Revenue Relevance

This blueprint supports monetization by making the next sample look exam-relevant without becoming legally fragile.

Revenue advantages:

- Stronger promise: `JFT-Basic / SSW exam-aligned practical Japanese`, not generic flashcards.
- Better landing copy: the product can mention four-section coverage, listening-ready practice, reading/information search, and work-life readiness.
- Better free-sample credibility: a 60-card Indonesian sample can show balanced exam coverage instead of random useful phrases.
- Clearer paid-pack path: expand from 60 validation cards to a 300-500 paid starter, then add Japanese audio and field-specific packs after demand evidence.
- Lower legal risk: public sources shape the curriculum, while all card content remains original.

Recommended next action after this blueprint:

1. Create a Stage 1B schema/content plan that supports Indonesian + English fallback and exam metadata.
2. Write only the first 60 original Stage 1B Indonesian validation cards after the schema direction is accepted.
3. Keep audio and PWA feature changes out of the next card-writing task unless explicitly approved.
4. Use learner/institution feedback to decide whether to expand Indonesian first or pivot to another reachable segment.
